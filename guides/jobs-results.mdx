Once a job is running, the job detail page becomes the main place to monitor progress and collect data.

## Understand job statuses

| Status | What it means | What you can do |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `pending` | The job was created and is waiting to start | Wait for processing to begin |
| `working` | The scraper is actively collecting data | Monitor progress and leave the page if needed |
| `ok` | The job completed successfully | Open result tabs, download CSV, or export |
| `failed` | The job did not finish successfully | Review the failure reason and retry with a smaller or simpler run |
| `aborting` | Cancellation has been requested | Wait for the job to stop |
| `cancelled` | The run was stopped before completion | Duplicate the setup in a new job if you still need the data |

## What you can do from the jobs list

From **Jobs**, you can:

- Search your existing jobs
- Open a job detail page
- Cancel an active job
- Delete a job you no longer need
- Download completed results
- Export completed results to Google Sheets

## Review the job detail page

The job detail page is organized around the result set and the final cost.

Common tabs include:

- **Overview** for the summary view
- **Contact** for phone, website, and contact-related fields
- **Ratings** for rating and review-count views
- **Reviews** for review text when review scraping was enabled
- **Costs** for the final cost breakdown

If a job is still running, some tabs stay empty until the run finishes.

## Download your CSV

When a job reaches `ok`, you can download a CSV version of the dataset.

Use CSV download when you want to:

- Open the file in spreadsheets locally
- Import it into your own tooling
- Keep an archive outside the dashboard

## Export to Google Sheets

BrezelScraper can send a completed job directly to Google Sheets.

Before you export:

1. Open **Integrations** in the dashboard
2. Connect your Google account
3. Return to the completed job
4. Choose **Export to Google Sheets**

You can set the sheet name before the export starts.

## Cancel vs delete

These actions do different things:

- **Cancel** stops an active job that is already running or queued
- **Delete** removes the job entry from your dashboard

Use **Cancel** when you want to stop more usage. Use **Delete** when you no longer need to keep the job in your account view.

## Why results might not appear

### The job is still running

Wait until the status changes to `ok`. Result tabs populate after successful completion.

### The job failed

Failed jobs do not behave like completed jobs. Review the error message, reduce complexity, and launch a new run.

### The job was cancelled

Cancelled jobs stop before the full dataset is ready. Re-run the search if you still need the output.

## Good habits for larger workloads

- Split broad searches into multiple smaller jobs
- Use naming conventions so you can find runs later
- Download or export completed jobs you plan to share
- Review costs on finished jobs before scaling the same setup
